\section{GT-free Outcome Signals}
\label{app:ground_truth_free_discrimination}

\subsection{Signal Definitions}
\label{app:subsec:gtfree_signal_definitions}

We define two ground-truth-free signals from the layer-wise diagnostic distributions.

\paragraph{CSD Entropy.}
The entropy of the CSD distribution at layer $\ell$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:csd_entropy}
H_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell = -\sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}} \Phi_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell[t] \log \Phi_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell[t].
\end{equation}
$H_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell$ remains high during brewing and drops sharply after resolution for Resolved and Misresolved samples, while staying elevated for Unresolved samples.

\paragraph{Probe--CSD Distributional Divergence.}
We quantify the probe--CSD discrepancy via Jensen--Shannon divergence:
\begin{align}
\label{eq:probe_csd_divergence}
D_{\mathrm{sym}}^\ell &= \frac{1}{2}\Big[\mathrm{KL}\big(\Phi_{\mathrm{P}}^\ell \,\|\, M^\ell\big) + \mathrm{KL}\big(\Phi_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell \,\|\, M^\ell\big)\Big], \nonumber \\
M^\ell &= \frac{1}{2}(\Phi_{\mathrm{P}}^\ell + \Phi_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell),
\end{align}
where $\mathrm{KL}(P\|Q) = \sum_t P[t]\log\frac{P[t]}{Q[t]}$.
Divergence is high during brewing (probing leads CSD), drops after resolution, and exhibits a characteristic non-monotonic pattern for Overprocessed samples (decrease at FJC followed by late-layer re-increase).

\subsection{Outcome-Specific Signal Characteristics}
\label{app:subsec:gtfree_outcome_signatures}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{GT-free outcome discrimination performance on the anchor setting (Qwen2.5-Coder-7B). Brewing AUC measures the ability to detect brewing-phase boundaries; OT AUC and OT Recall measure Overprocessed detection; Overall Agree is the four-way agreement with true outcome labels.}
\label{tab:app:gtfree_performance}
\resizebox{\columnwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lccccc}
\toprule
Signal & Brewing AUC & OT AUC & OT Recall & Overall Agree \\
\midrule
$H_{\mathrm{C}}^\ell$ only        & 0.988 & 0.425 & ---   & 50\% \\
Non-digit signal only              & ---   & 0.915 & 83.1\% & 60\% \\
Combined                           & 0.988 & 0.915 & 83.1\% & \textbf{87.0\%} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}%
}
\end{table}

CSD entropy alone achieves near-perfect brewing detection (AUC 0.988) but cannot distinguish Overprocessed from Resolved, since both show low entropy after FJC.
The distributional divergence complements this by capturing late-layer re-divergence (OT AUC 0.915, recall 83.1\%).
Combined, the two signals reach 87.0\% four-way agreement with true outcome labels (\cref{tab:app:gtfree_performance}), demonstrating that the brewing-to-resolution taxonomy is largely recoverable from internal signals alone.
