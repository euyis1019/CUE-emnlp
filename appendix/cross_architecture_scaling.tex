\subsection{Coder vs.\ Base Paired Analysis}
\label{app:subsec:coder_vs_base}

We compare Qwen2.5-Coder-7B with Qwen2.5-7B (same architecture, $L{=}28$, no code-specialized training).
\cref{tab:app:coder_vs_base_computing} reports results on Computing.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Coder vs.\ Base on Computing: outcome distribution and brewing
layer positions for Qwen2.5-Coder-7B and Qwen2.5-7B ($L{=}28$).
Normalized depths in parentheses.}
\label{tab:app:coder_vs_base_computing}
\resizebox{\columnwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lccccccc}
\toprule
Model & Res.\% & OT\% & MR\% & UR\% & FPCL & FJC & $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$ \\
\midrule
Qwen2.5-Coder-7B & 33.5 & 45.9 & 12.4 & 8.1 & 9.6 (34\%) & 20.8 (74\%) & 11.2 (40\%) \\
Qwen2.5-7B       & 20.5 & 64.1 & 3.8 & 11.5 & 10.1 (36\%) & 20.5 (73\%) & 10.4 (37\%) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}%
}
\end{table}

Code-specialized training substantially improves the Resolved rate (33.5\% vs.\ 20.5\%), confirming enhanced arithmetic \emph{capability}.
However, normalized FPCL and FJC positions remain similar (FPCL: 34\% vs.\ 36\%; FJC: 74\% vs.\ 73\%), indicating that the processing \emph{schedule} is architecture-determined rather than training-data-determined.

\subsection{Scaling Trends}
\label{app:subsec:scaling_trends}

\cref{tab:app:scaling_computing} reports how outcome distributions and brewing dynamics change across model scale (0.5B--14B) on Computing.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Scaling trends on Computing for Qwen2.5-Coder (0.5B--14B).
$L$ denotes the number of transformer layers; norm.\ $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$
is the brewing duration normalized by total depth.}
\label{tab:app:scaling_computing}
\resizebox{\columnwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lcccccr}
\toprule
Scale & $L$ & Res.\% & OT\% & MR\% & UR\% & norm.\ $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$\% \\
\midrule
0.5B  & 24 & 12 & 18 & 22 & 48 & 42 \\
1.5B  & 28 & 22 & 38 & 18 & 22 & 40 \\
3B    & 36 & 28 & 42 & 16 & 14 & 41 \\
7B    & 28 & 33.5 & 45.9 & 12.4 & 8.1 & 40 \\
14B   & 48 & 55 & 30 & 8 & 7 & 39 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}%
}
\end{table}

Several trends emerge:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Resolved increases with scale}, consistent with improved arithmetic capability.

\item \textbf{Unresolved decreases monotonically}, suggesting larger models develop richer internal representations even for incorrectly answered instances.

\item \textbf{Overprocessed shows a non-monotonic trend}, peaking at intermediate scale before declining---mid-sized models can \emph{represent} the answer but lack robustness to \emph{maintain} it through remaining layers.

\item \textbf{Normalized brewing duration remains stable} at approximately 40\% of total depth across scales ($L=24$ to $L=48$), suggesting this proportion is an architectural invariant.
\end{enumerate}


\subsection{Cross-Architecture Robustness}
\label{app:subsec:cross_architecture_robustness}

To test generalization beyond the Qwen family, we apply the same pipeline to DeepSeek-Coder-6.7B, CodeLlama-7B, and Llama-3.1-8B.
\cref{tab:app:cross_arch_computing} reports Computing-task results.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Cross-architecture results on Computing.  All three non-Qwen
models exhibit the four-way outcome split and identifiable FPCL/FJC
positions.  Normalized depths in parentheses.}
\label{tab:app:cross_arch_computing}
\resizebox{\columnwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lcccccc}
\toprule
Model & Res.\% & OT\% & MR\% & UR\% & FPCL (norm.) & FJC (norm.) \\
\midrule
DeepSeek-Coder-6.7B & 30 & 42 & 15 & 13 & 10.2 (32\%) & 17.9 (56\%) \\
CodeLlama-7B        & 25 & 40 & 18 & 17 & 10.6 (33\%) & 17.6 (55\%) \\
Llama-3.1-8B        & 22 & 38 & 20 & 20 & 11.2 (35\%) & 18.2 (57\%) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}%
}
\end{table}

Three findings generalize across architectures:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Brewing universally exists.}
  All models exhibit nonzero $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$, confirming that the brewing phenomenon is not Qwen-specific.

\item \textbf{Task-dependent FPCL ordering is preserved.}
  Computing consistently shows a later FPCL than control-flow tasks across all architectures, mirroring the Qwen-family pattern (\cref{app:subsec:fpcl_fjc_layer_positions}).

\item \textbf{Absolute performance varies but structural fingerprints are consistent.}
  Code-specialized models achieve higher Resolved rates than general-purpose Llama-3.1-8B, paralleling the Coder-vs-Base gap (\cref{app:subsec:coder_vs_base}), while the relative FPCL/FJC ordering across tasks remains stable.
\end{itemize}
