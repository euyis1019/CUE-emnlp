Two observations emerge.
First, code-specialized training substantially improves the Resolved rate
(29.4\% vs.\ [XX]\%) and reduces Unresolved outcomes (16.8\% vs.\ [XX]\%),
confirming that continued pre-training on code enhances \emph{capability} for
arithmetic reasoning within code contexts.
Second, the normalized FPCL and FJC positions remain similar across Coder
and Base variants (FPCL: 34\% vs.\ [XX]\%; FJC: 74\% vs.\ [XX]\%),
indicating that the \emph{temporal scaffold} of information processing---when
probing first detects the answer and when CSD commits to it---is largely
determined by the shared architecture rather than by domain-specific training.
This supports a separation between \emph{mechanism} (layer-wise processing
schedule) and \emph{capability} (task success rate).

% 定性描述四个趋势，保持保守措辞。
Several trends are apparent from \cref{tab:app:scaling_computing}:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Resolved increases with scale.}
  Larger models solve a greater fraction of Computing instances, consistent
  with improved arithmetic capability at larger parameter counts.

\item \textbf{Unresolved decreases monotonically.}
  The fraction of samples where the model never reaches the correct answer
  (neither via probing nor CSD) shrinks steadily, suggesting that larger
  models develop richer internal representations even for instances they
  ultimately answer incorrectly.

\item \textbf{Overprocessed shows a non-monotonic trend.}
  The Overprocessed rate---where the correct answer appears in probing but is
  subsequently overwritten---peaks at intermediate scale before declining.
  This is consistent with the hypothesis that mid-sized models develop
  sufficient capacity to \emph{represent} the answer but lack the
  robustness to \emph{maintain} it through the remaining layers; larger models
  resolve this tension.

\item \textbf{Normalized brewing duration remains stable.}
  Despite variation in absolute layer counts ($L=24$ to $L=48$), the
  normalized $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$ stays approximately [XX]\% $\pm$
  [X.X]\%.
  This stability implies that the proportional depth devoted to the
  brewing-to-resolution transition is an architectural invariant rather than a
  scale-dependent quantity.
\end{enumerate}

% 定性总结：哪些方面一致，哪些方面有差异。
Three findings generalize across architectures:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Brewing universally exists.}
  All models exhibit a nonzero $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$: probing detects the
  correct answer before CSD commits to it, confirming that the
  brewing phenomenon is not specific to Qwen's architecture or training data.

\item \textbf{Task-dependent FPCL ordering is preserved.}
  Computing consistently shows a later FPCL (higher normalized depth) than
  control-flow tasks across all architectures, mirroring the pattern observed
  for the Qwen family (\cref{app:subsec:fpcl_fjc_layer_positions}).
  This suggests that the relative difficulty ordering among task families is
  an intrinsic property of how transformers process code, not an artifact
  of any single model's training.

\item \textbf{Absolute performance varies.}
  Code-specialized models (DeepSeek-Coder, CodeLlama) tend to achieve higher
  Resolved rates on Computing than the general-purpose Llama-3.1-8B,
  paralleling the Coder-vs-Base gap observed in \cref{app:subsec:coder_vs_base}.
  However, the \emph{structural fingerprint}---the relative ordering of FPCL
  and FJC across tasks---remains consistent regardless of absolute performance
  level.
\end{itemize}
