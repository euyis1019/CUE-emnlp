Cross-task variation in $\Delta_{\mathrm{brew}}$ is thus driven by FPCL differences rather than FJC: tasks that are easier to represent internally begin brewing earlier, but all converge to a similar resolution depth.

\subsection{FJC Existence and Final Correctness}
\label{app:subsec:fjc_correctness_correlation}

FJC absence is a near-necessary indicator of failure: only 0.65\% of samples with $\mathrm{FJC} = \varnothing$ produce a correct output.
Among samples with $\mathrm{FJC} \neq \varnothing$, 84.0\% are Resolved while 16.0\% are Overprocessed, confirming that FJC marks a genuine computational milestone rather than a statistical artifact.
